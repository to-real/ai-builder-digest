## No Priors — Why Traditional Benchmarks Fail Modern AI Models with OpenAI Research Scientist Noam Brown

*No Priors*

**Model capability is now a function of budget, not just architecture, rendering static benchmarks obsolete.**

The Takeaway: We must evaluate AI models by plotting performance against compute cost, not static scores. Noam Brown, an OpenAI Research Scientist known for his work on reasoning, argues that the industry is stuck in a "bad equilibrium" of static benchmark grids. With models like GPT-5.5 capable of improving performance over weeks of computation or millions of tokens, a single score is meaningless. Brown explains that "the capability of the model is a function of how much money you put into it," meaning safety evaluations and capability assessments must define a budget to be accurate. He warns that current responsible scaling policies ignore this variable, potentially missing dangerous capabilities that only unlock at high inference spend. While models aren't yet replacing researchers due to a lack of "taste," Brown believes we are heading toward a gradual takeoff where time, not just intelligence, becomes the primary bottleneck.

The Takeaway: 我们必须通过绘制性能与计算成本的关系图来评估 AI 模型，而不是静态分数。OpenAI 研究科学家 Noam Brown 以其在推理方面的工作而闻名，他认为行业陷入了静态基准测试网格的“糟糕均衡”。随着像 GPT-5.5 这样的模型能够在数周的计算或数百万 token 中持续提高性能，单一分数毫无意义。Brown 解释说，“模型的能力取决于你投入多少资金”，这意味着安全评估和能力评估必须定义预算才能准确。他警告说，当前的责任扩展政策忽略了这一变量，可能会错过只有在高推理支出下才能解锁的危险能力。虽然模型由于缺乏“品味”尚未取代研究人员，但 Brown 认为我们正走向一个渐进的起飞，其中时间而不仅仅是智能成为主要的瓶颈。
