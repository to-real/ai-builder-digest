## No Priors — Really Big Test-Time Compute in AI Changes Benchmarks, Safety and Research with OpenAI Research Scientist Noam Brown

*No Priors*

**Current benchmarks and safety frameworks are obsolete because they treat capability as a fixed attribute rather than a function of compute budget and time.**

当前的基准测试和安全框架已经过时，因为它们将能力视为固定属性，而非计算预算和时间的函数。

Noam Brown, an OpenAI Research Scientist known for his work on AI reasoning, argues that the industry is stuck in a "bad equilibrium" of evaluating models using static benchmark grids. With modern models like GPT-5.5, capability scales indefinitely with test-time compute on reasoning tasks—unlike factual recall, which plateaus quickly. This renders single-number benchmark scores misleading; a model can appear worse on a grid but outperform others when given a larger budget. Brown warns that existing safety evaluations are similarly flawed because they don't account for the fact that a model's dangerous potential is unlocked by money and time, not just intelligence. He predicts a gradual takeoff rather than an overnight explosion, noting that "the capability of the model is a function of how much money you put into it," making time the ultimate bottleneck for recursive self-improvement.

OpenAI 研究科学家 Noam Brown 以其在 AI 推理方面的工作而闻名，他认为行业陷入了使用静态基准网格评估模型的“糟糕均衡”。对于像 GPT-5.5 这样的现代模型，推理任务上的能力会随着测试时计算无限扩展——这与很快就会趋于平稳的事实检索不同。这使得单一数字的基准分数具有误导性；模型在网格上可能看起来更差，但在获得更大预算时却能超越其他模型。Brown 警告说，现有的安全评估同样存在缺陷，因为它们没有考虑到模型的危险潜力是由金钱和时间解锁的，而不仅仅是智能。他预测是渐进式起飞而非一夜之间的爆发，并指出“模型的能力是你投入多少资金的函数”，这使得时间成为递归自我改进的终极瓶颈。
