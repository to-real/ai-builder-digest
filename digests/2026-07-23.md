**The future of software development is autonomous "dark factories" where agents work asynchronously, and success depends on model-agnostic routing rather than betting on a single provider.**

Matan Grinberg, CEO of Factory, argues that we are moving toward "dark factories" in software development—environments where autonomous agents build software asynchronously without human intervention, similar to Tesla's manufacturing lines. A key insight is that enterprises are terrified of vendor lock-in after the cloud wars; they want model independence so they can swap out models like Opus or GPT 5.6 based on cost and performance. Grinberg predicts that within 12-24 months, 90% of token usage will be asynchronous as agents proactively fix issues rather than just responding to chat prompts. He also shares a radical story about refunding $2M in revenue because the product wasn't creating "obsessed customers," emphasizing that output matters more than input metrics like "customer obsession." "Our job is to build something so good that our customers themselves become obsessed with us."

Factory CEO Matan Grinberg 认为，软件开发正迈向“暗工厂”时代——即自主 Agent 在没有人工干预的情况下异步构建软件的环境，类似于特斯拉的生产线。一个关键洞察是，企业在经历云服务大战后对厂商锁定感到恐惧，他们需要模型独立性，以便根据成本和性能灵活切换 Opus 或 GPT 5.6 等模型。Grinberg 预测，在未来 12-24 个月内，90% 的 token 使用将是异步的，因为 Agent 将主动修复问题而不仅仅是响应聊天提示。他还分享了一个激进的故事：因为产品没有创造“痴迷的客户”，公司退还了 200 万美元的收入，强调输出结果比“客户痴迷”这样的输入指标更重要。“我们的工作是构建好到让客户对我们痴迷的产品。”
