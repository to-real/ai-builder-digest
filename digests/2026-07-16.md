## Training Data — Anthropic's Katelyn Lesse & Angela Jiang: Building an Ecosystem, not a Walled Garden

*Training Data Podcast*

**Anthropic is building a layered platform stack—knowledge, execution, and coordination—prioritizing open standards like MCP to let the ecosystem define the future form factors of AI, rather than locking users into a walled garden.**

Katelyn Lesse and Angela Jiang, who lead Anthropic's Platform team, discuss the evolution of their developer infrastructure. They describe a "layer cake" of abstractions: the bottom layer is knowledge (APIs, context), the middle is execution (managed agents, infrastructure), and the emerging top layer is coordination (strategies). They argue that as models become more steerable, the role of the "harness" shifts from guiding the model to managing long-running workflows and token allocation. A key insight is their philosophy of openness; they launch products like Claude Design or Team Tag to demonstrate potential form factors but ultimately want to provide primitives (like MCP and skills) that allow the market to innovate. They emphasize that the most exciting innovation is happening at the "context and connectivity layer," where companies are ingeniously routing data between legacy systems and agents. Regarding the "token rationalization" trend, they advise against simply capping usage, suggesting instead that companies design routing strategies to match task complexity with the appropriate model size.

Anthropic 正在构建一个分层平台堆栈——知识、执行和协调——优先考虑像 MCP 这样的开放标准，让生态系统定义 AI 的未来形态，而不是将用户锁定在围墙花园中。

Katelyn Lesse 和 Angela Jiang 领导 Anthropic 的平台团队，他们讨论了开发者基础设施的演变。他们描述了一个“分层蛋糕”式的抽象结构：底层是知识（API、上下文），中间层是执行（托管 agent、基础设施），而正在出现的顶层是协调（策略）。他们认为，随着模型变得更易操控，“harness”的角色从引导模型转向管理长期工作流和 token 分配。一个关键的见解是他们的开放理念；他们推出 Claude Design 或 Team Tag 等产品是为了展示潜在的形态，但最终希望提供原语（如 MCP 和 skills），让市场进行创新。他们强调，最令人兴奋的创新发生在“上下文和连接层”，公司正在巧妙地在遗留系统和 agent 之间路由数据。关于“token 理性化”趋势，他们建议简单地限制使用是不可取的，而是建议公司设计路由策略，将任务复杂度与适当的模型大小相匹配。
