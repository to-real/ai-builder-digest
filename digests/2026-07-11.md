## Unsupervised Learning — Ep 90: AI Pioneer Jürgen Schmidhuber on the State of AI Today

*Unsupervised Learning*

**True AGI requires physical robots, not just screen-bound models, and the current trillion-dollar GPU investment bubble is destined to burst.**

Jürgen Schmidhuber, often called the father of modern AI, argues that while today's large language models pass the Turing test, they are merely "fancy text editors" compared to the physical AI needed for true general intelligence. He emphasizes that robot hardware, particularly the dexterity of the human hand, remains vastly inferior to biological evolution and is the real bottleneck. Schmidhuber predicts a shift from human-biased models trained on the web to "artificial scientists" that generate their own data through curiosity and experimentation, much like babies learn physics. On the business side, he is deeply pessimistic about model companies, warning that the current capital expenditure boom is a misallocation of resources. He points out that compute costs drop by a factor of ten every five years, rendering today's massive GPU investments obsolete and unprofitable, especially as open source models rapidly catch up to closed-source frontier models. Regarding safety, he dismisses "Terminator" scenarios, suggesting that super-intelligent AIs will be motivated to protect humanity as the source of their own origins and interesting patterns.

真正的 AGI 需要物理机器人，而不仅仅是屏幕上的模型，当前万亿美元级的 GPU 投资泡沫注定会破裂，因为算力正在商品化且开源正在侵蚀利润。

被称为现代 AI 之父的 Jürgen Schmidhuber 认为，虽然今天的大语言模型通过了图灵测试，但与真正的通用智能所需的物理 AI 相比，它们仅仅是“花哨的文本编辑器”。他强调，机器人硬件——尤其是人类手的灵巧度——仍然远逊于生物进化，这才是真正的瓶颈。Schmidhuber 预测，AI 将从基于网络数据训练的、带有强烈人类偏见的模型，转向通过好奇心和实验生成自己数据的“人工科学家”，就像婴儿学习物理一样。在商业方面，他对模型公司深感悲观，警告当前的资本支出热潮是对资源的错误配置。他指出，算力成本每五年下降十倍，这使得今天大规模的 GPU 投资变得过时且无利可图，尤其是当开源模型迅速追赶闭源前沿模型时。关于安全，他驳斥了“终结者”式的场景，认为超级智能 AI 将有动力保护人类，因为人类是它们自身起源和有趣模式的来源。
