## No Priors — Travel Through the Lens of AI with Booking.com CEO Glenn Fogel

*No Priors*

**The Takeaway:** AI will revolutionize travel planning through personalized agents, but the industry's operational complexity and regulatory landscape remain significant barriers to entry.

Glenn Fogel, CEO of Booking Holdings, shares how his company survived the dot-com crash and grew from a $100M valuation to over $100B by constantly adapting. He argues that while AI agents like "Penny" can handle complex multi-destination trips and optimize costs, the real challenge isn't the technology—it's the "plumbing" of connecting with thousands of partners and navigating global regulations. Fogel warns against the illusion of moats: "There is no such thing as a moat... The only way to win long term is to continue to develop new services." He also addresses the societal impact of AI, noting that while technology creates jobs, the speed of displacement requires urgent conversations about upskilling and avoiding a public backlash that could stifle innovation.

**核心观点：** AI 将通过个性化 agent 彻底改变旅行规划，但该行业的运营复杂性和监管格局仍然是进入的重要壁垒。Booking Holdings CEO Glenn Fogel 分享了他的公司如何在互联网泡沫破裂中幸存，并通过不断适应将估值从 1 亿美元增长到超过 1000 亿美元。他认为，虽然像 "Penny" 这样的 AI agent 可以处理复杂的多目的地旅行并优化成本，但真正的挑战不在于技术，而在于与数千家合作伙伴连接以及应对全球监管的“管道工程”。Fogel 警告不要对护城河抱有幻想：“不存在所谓的护城河……长期获胜的唯一途径是继续开发新服务。”他还谈到了 AI 的社会影响，指出虽然技术创造了就业机会，但替代的速度需要我们就技能提升和避免可能扼杀创新的公众强烈反对进行紧急对话。
