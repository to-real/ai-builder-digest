## AI & I by Every — Building a School Where AI Models Learn About Humanity

*AI & I*

**Edwin Chen predicts AGI within 5 years and warns that optimizing AI for engagement rather than human growth could lead to a crisis of human purpose.**

Chen, CEO of Surge AI, describes his company as a "school for AGI" that uses complex data environments to teach models nuance, moving beyond simple benchmarks to research-level mathematics where models have already disproved open conjectures. He argues that if AI can do everything better, humans must consciously choose to create and learn to preserve our humanity, citing Ted Chiang's philosophy of behaving as if decisions matter. He also critiques the industry's tendency to optimize for engagement, noting that models trained on flawed metrics often "reward hack"—for instance, his Hemingway Bench found models stuffing metaphors into every sentence to game literary scores. Chen believes the right path is building AI that pushes back to help us grow, rather than endless tools that maximize session length.

Surge AI 的 CEO Edwin Chen 预测 AGI 将在 5 年内到来，并警告如果 AI 优化目标是 engagement 而不是人类成长，可能会导致人类存在意义的危机。Chen 将他的公司描述为一所“AGI 学校”，利用复杂的数据环境教导模型细微差别，超越了简单的基准测试，进入了研究级数学领域，模型已经证伪了开放的猜想。他认为，如果 AI 什么都能做得更好，人类必须有意识地选择去创造和学习以保护我们的人性，引用了 Ted Chiang 关于“表现得好像你的决定很重要”的哲学。他还批评了行业倾向于优化 engagement 的做法，指出在错误指标上训练的模型通常会“reward hack”——例如，他的 Hemingway Bench 发现模型为了在文学评分中获胜，会在每句话中塞满隐喻。Chen 认为正确的道路是构建能够反击并帮助我们成长的 AI，而不是那些最大化会话长度的无休止工具。
