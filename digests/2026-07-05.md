## The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — Why NVIDIA Is Giving Away AI Models | Bryan Catanzaro

*The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck*

**NVIDIA builds open models like Nemotron not to compete, but to understand how to build better hardware and to support the ecosystem that drives their GPU business.**

NVIDIA 构建像 Nemotron 这样的开源模型不是为了竞争，而是为了理解如何构建更好的硬件，并支持推动其 GPU 业务的生态系统。

Bryan Catanzaro, VP of Applied Research at NVIDIA, argues that the company's investment in open-source models is driven by two necessities: understanding how to build future hardware systems and supporting the ecosystem that buys their GPUs. With Moore's Law dead, NVIDIA believes the only way to scale intelligence is through radical efficiency, leading to innovations like 4-bit pretraining and Mixture of Experts (MoE) architectures that allow models to run at the limit of power and economic constraints. Catanzaro champions open source as the safest path for AI, contrasting the "sunlight" of community scrutiny with the risks of monoculture, and emphasizes that NVIDIA's unique culture—"mission is the boss"—enables hundreds of researchers to collaborate effectively on a single model using techniques like multi-teacher distillation. He dismisses the "singularity" narrative, suggesting intelligence is multifaceted and context-dependent, and views AI as an "external brain" that will profoundly change how we solve planetary problems.

NVIDIA 应用研究副总裁 Bryan Catanzaro 认为，公司投资 Nemotron 等开源模型源于两个必要性：了解如何构建未来的硬件系统，以及支持购买其 GPU 的生态系统。随着摩尔定律的失效，NVIDIA 认为扩展智能的唯一途径是通过极致的效率，这推动了 4-bit 预训练和混合专家（MoE）架构等创新，使模型能够在电力和经济约束的极限下运行。Catanzaro 拥护开源作为 AI 最安全的路径，将社区审查的“阳光”与单一文化的风险进行对比，并强调 NVIDIA 独特的文化——“使命即老板”——使数百名研究人员能够利用多教师蒸馏等技术有效地协作构建单一模型。他驳斥了“奇点”叙事，认为智能是多方面且依赖情境的，并将 AI 视为将深刻改变我们解决全球问题方式的“外脑”。
