## The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries
*The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck*
**Reinforcement learning is now the core driver of AI reasoning, allowing models to autonomously make scientific discoveries through test-time thinking.**
Dan Roberts, who leads the foundations of reinforcement learning team at OpenAI, argues that RL is now the "cake" rather than the cherry, driving the shift from models that merely predict text to systems that can reason and discover. He explains that recent breakthroughs in solving complex math problems like the Erdos conjectures stem from models' ability to explore contrarian paths and think for hours at test time. Roberts applies a physicist's lens to AI scaling, advocating for a "big to small" approach where complex emergent behaviors are reverse-engineered into simpler, understandable laws rather than accepted as magic. He predicts a smooth acceleration in AI's role in science, noting that while models can already automate parts of research, the future lies in systems that can autonomously formulate the right questions, not just solve defined problems.
强化学习已从辅助技术演变为 AI 推理的核心引擎，使模型能够通过测试时的思考自主进行科学发现。OpenAI 强化学习基础团队负责人 Dan Roberts 认为，RL 现在是“蛋糕”而不仅仅是“樱桃”，推动了模型从仅仅预测文本转向能够推理和发现的系统。他解释说，最近在解决 Erdos 猜想等复杂数学问题方面的突破，源于模型在测试时探索反直觉路径并思考数小时的能力。Roberts 用物理学家的视角审视 AI 扩展，主张“从大到小”的方法，即通过逆向工程将复杂的涌现行为简化为可理解的规律，而不是将其视为魔法。他预测 AI 在科学中的作用将平稳加速，指出虽然模型已经可以自动化的部分研究工作，但未来的关键在于能够自主提出正确问题的系统，而不仅仅是解决定义好的问题。
