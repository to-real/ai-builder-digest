## The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries

*The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck*

**AI has transitioned from a tool that follows instructions to an active participant in scientific discovery, driven by the shift from pre-training to reinforcement learning.**

Dan Roberts, a theoretical physicist turned OpenAI researcher, explains how reinforcement learning (RL) has evolved from a "cherry on top" to the main "cake" of AI development. He contrasts OpenAI's informal reasoning approach with DeepMind's formal Lean-based methods for solving math problems, highlighting how models now possess the "strong conviction" to pursue contrarian paths that human mathematicians might avoid. Roberts argues that scaling laws should be understood "big to small"—using insights from massive systems to restore smoothness in smaller ones, much like physicists simplify complex models. He believes the internet provides a necessary linguistic grounding for intelligence, disagreeing with the idea that pure RL is sufficient. Ultimately, he predicts a smooth, gradual integration of AI into science, where models act as tireless explorers capable of synthesizing disparate fields to solve problems like the Erdos conjecture.

Dan Roberts，一位从理论物理学家转型的 OpenAI 研究员，解释了强化学习（RL）如何从“蛋糕上的樱桃”演变为 AI 开发的“蛋糕本身”。他对比了 OpenAI 的非形式化推理方法与 DeepMind 基于形式化语言 Lean 的数学解题方法，强调了模型现在拥有人类数学家可能回避的“坚定信念”，去追求反直觉的路径。Roberts 认为应该从“大到小”来理解缩放定律——利用从巨型系统中获得的洞察来恢复小系统的平滑性，就像物理学家简化复杂模型一样。他相信互联网为智能提供了必要的语言基础，不同意纯 RL 就足够的观点。最终，他预测 AI 将平滑、渐进地融入科学领域，模型将成为不知疲倦的探索者，能够综合不同领域的知识来解决像 Erdos 猜想这样的问题。
