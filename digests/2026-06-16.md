## The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries

*The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck*

**Reinforcement learning is no longer the cherry on top; it is the entire cake driving scientific discovery.**

Dan Roberts, who leads the foundations of reinforcement learning team at OpenAI, argues that while pre-training provides a necessary linguistic prior, the breakthrough in scientific capabilities comes from RL enabling models to "think" at test time. He contrasts OpenAI's approach to solving the Erdos unit distance problem—using informal reasoning and long chains of thought—with DeepMind's formal Lean-based proofs, noting that OpenAI's model succeeded by taking a contrarian stance (assuming a conjecture was false) and persisting through a long calculation path. Roberts challenges the idea that capabilities "emerge" at scale, advocating instead for a physics-based approach where we understand complex systems by simplifying them to restore smoothness in scaling laws. He believes RL allows models to explore and exploit in ways that pure pre-training cannot, turning compute into genuine reasoning capability.

OpenAI 强化学习基础团队负责人 Dan Roberts 认为，虽然预训练提供了必要的语言先验，但科学能力的突破来自于 RL 使模型能够在测试时进行“思考”。他对比了 OpenAI 解决 Erdos 单位距离问题的方法（使用非形式推理和长思维链）与 DeepMind 基于形式化 Lean 的证明，指出 OpenAI 的模型通过采取反直觉立场（假设猜想是错误的）并在漫长的计算路径中坚持下来而取得了成功。Roberts 挑战了能力在规模上“涌现”的观点，提倡一种基于物理学的方法，即通过简化复杂系统来恢复扩展定律的平滑度。他认为 RL 允许模型以纯预训练无法实现的方式进行探索和利用，将算力转化为真正的推理能力。
