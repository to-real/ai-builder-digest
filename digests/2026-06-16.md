**Anthropic introduces "dreaming" for self-improving agents and multiagent orchestration in Managed Agents.**

## The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries

*The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck*

**Reinforcement learning has evolved from a supplementary technique to the core engine of AI intelligence, enabling models to perform genuine scientific discovery through contrarian reasoning.**

Dan Roberts, a theoretical physicist leading the Foundations of Reinforcement Learning team at OpenAI, discusses the intersection of AI and science, particularly how models are now solving long-standing mathematical problems like the Erdos conjectures. Roberts explains that RL is no longer just the "cherry on top" but the "cake" itself, essential for converting compute into reasoning capabilities. He highlights how OpenAI's models differ from DeepMind's formal approach by using informal language to reason, allowing them to be contrarian—assuming a conjecture is false and persisting through complex calculations to disprove it. He also shares a physics-inspired perspective on scaling laws, arguing that "emergence" is just a sign we didn't understand the system at smaller scales, and that true understanding comes from restoring smoothness from big to small.

Dan Roberts 是一位理论物理学家，目前领导 OpenAI 的强化学习基础团队。他讨论了 AI 与科学的交叉点，特别是模型如何解决长期存在的数学问题，如 Erdos 猜想。Roberts 解释说，RL 不再仅仅是“锦上添花”，而是“蛋糕”本身，对于将算力转化为推理能力至关重要。他强调 OpenAI 的模型与 DeepMind 的形式化方法不同，它们使用非正式语言进行推理，这使它们能够表现出反直觉的特性——假设某个猜想是错误的，并通过复杂的计算坚持反驳它。他还分享了一个受物理学启发的扩展定律视角，认为“涌现”只是我们在小尺度上不理解系统的标志，真正的理解来自于从大到小恢复平滑性。
