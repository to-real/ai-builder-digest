## Claude Blog: New in Claude Managed Agents: dreaming, outcomes, and multiagent orchestration

**Anthropic is introducing "dreaming" to help agents self-improve by reviewing past sessions to extract patterns and refine their own memory.**

Dreaming is a scheduled process that surfaces recurring mistakes, workflow convergences, and team preferences that a single agent might miss, restructuring memory to maintain high signal over time. The update also launches "outcomes," which uses a separate grader model to evaluate work against a user-defined rubric, improving task success by up to 10 points on hard problems. Additionally, multiagent orchestration allows a lead agent to delegate tasks to specialist sub-agents with their own models and tools, enabling parallel processing of complex jobs like legal drafting or log analysis.

“Dreaming”是一个定期流程，能发现单个 Agent 可能错过的重复错误、工作流收敛和团队偏好，从而重构记忆以保持高信噪比。此次更新还推出了“outcomes”，它使用独立的评分模型根据用户定义的标准来评估工作，在难题上将任务成功率提高了高达 10 分。此外，multiagent orchestration 允许主 Agent 将任务委派给拥有自己模型和工具的专业子 Agent，从而实现法律起草或日志分析等复杂任务的并行处理。

## The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries

*The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck*

**Reinforcement learning is the primary engine for converting compute into intelligence, enabling models to "think" at test time rather than just predict tokens.**

Dan Roberts, who leads the foundations of reinforcement learning team at OpenAI, explains that the paradigm has shifted from pre-training to reasoning, where models spend compute during inference to explore solutions and verify results. He cites the recent solution to the Erdos problem as an example of AI being contrarian—assuming a conjecture was false—and persisting through a long calculation path that required cross-domain expertise in algebraic number theory. Roberts also applies a physicist's lens to AI scaling, arguing that "emergence" is often a sign that we don't understand the small scale yet; true scientific understanding comes from simplifying complex systems until their behavior becomes smooth and predictable, rather than accepting discontinuities at scale.

OpenAI 强化学习基础团队负责人 Dan Roberts 解释说，范式已经从预训练转向推理，模型在推理过程中花费算力来探索解决方案并验证结果。他以最近 Erdos 问题的解决方案为例，说明了 AI 的反直觉能力——假设某个猜想是错误的——并坚持需要代数数论跨领域专业知识的漫长计算路径。Roberts 还用物理学家的视角来看待 AI 的扩展，认为“涌现”通常是我们尚未理解小规模系统的迹象；真正的科学理解来自于简化复杂系统直到其行为变得平滑可预测，而不是接受大规模下的不连续性。
