## Anthropic Engineering: How we contain Claude across products

Anthropic details their security architecture for agentic products, explaining how they use ephemeral containers, sandboxes, and VMs to cap the "blast radius" of Claude across claude.ai, Claude Code, and Cowork. The post highlights that while model-layer defenses like classifiers are strong, they are never 100% effective, making environment-layer containment critical. They share lessons learned from real incidents, such as phishing attacks and data exfiltration through approved domains, emphasizing that deterministic boundaries are what catch failures when probabilistic defenses miss.

Anthropic 详细介绍了其 Agent 产品的安全架构，解释了他们如何使用临时容器、沙箱和虚拟机来限制 Claude 在 claude.ai、Claude Code 和 Cowork 中的“爆炸半径”。文章强调，虽然分类器等模型层防御很强，但永远无法达到 100% 的有效性，因此环境层隔离至关重要。他们分享了从真实事件中学到的教训，例如网络钓鱼攻击和通过批准域名进行的数据泄露，强调当概率性防御失效时，确定性边界才是捕获失败的关键。

## Unsupervised Learning — Ep 91: Top AI Analyst Unpacks Today's AI Hype Cycle

*Unsupervised Learning*

**Benedict Evans:** AI is likely as big as the internet or mobile, but we shouldn't ignore historical patterns from previous technological shifts like electricity or PCs.

Benedict Evans：AI 可能像互联网或移动技术一样重大，但我们不应忽略电力或 PC 等以前技术转变的历史模式。

Benedict Evans challenges the "civilization-altering" hype around AI, arguing that while it's a major platform shift, we can learn a lot by looking at how electricity, mobile, and the internet actually played out. He points out that consumer AI applications currently lack broad product-market fit outside of coding, and critiques lab leaders for predicting job displacement without understanding what those jobs actually entail—citing Geoffrey Hinton's famous error about radiologists. Evans suggests that the "jagged edge" of model capabilities and the lack of network effects for LLMs mean the value might accrue differently than many expect, potentially more to the application layer than the model providers themselves. He warns against "Anselm's proof" style arguments where people define AGI as inevitable and then argue it will happen, urging instead to focus on what we can actually build and measure today.

Benedict Evans 挑战了关于 AI“改变文明”的炒作，认为虽然这是一个主要的平台转变，但我们可以通过观察电力、移动和互联网的实际发展学到很多东西。他指出，除了编程之外，消费者 AI 应用目前缺乏广泛的产品市场契合度，并批评实验室领导者在不了解工作实际内容的情况下预测工作岗位将被取代——引用了 Geoffrey Hinton 关于放射科医生的著名错误。Evans 认为，模型能力的“参差不齐”和 LLM 缺乏网络效应意味着价值的积累方式可能与许多人预期的不同，可能更多流向应用层而非模型提供商本身。他警告不要使用“安瑟伦证明”式的论点，即人们将 AGI 定义为不可避免的然后论证它会发生，而是敦促专注于我们今天实际可以构建和测量的东西。
